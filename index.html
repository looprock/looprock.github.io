Yes, “working people” is a shibboleth in U.S. political discourse, particularly among progressive and labor-oriented politicians. It’s often used as a way to signal alignment with workers, unions, and the middle or lower class without explicitly saying “working class,” which can carry more rigid or ideological connotations.
